## Problem 2. Systems of Equations (Step-by-Step Solution)

Given the system of equations:

$$
2x + 3y = 12 \quad \text{(Equation 1)}
$$

$$
x - y = 1 \quad \text{(Equation 2)}
$$

### Step 1: Express one variable in terms of the other
From Equation 2, we can easily isolate $x$:

$$
x = y + 1 \quad \text{(Equation 3)}
$$

### Step 2: Substitute this expression into the other equation
Substitute Equation 3 into Equation 1:

$$
2(y + 1) + 3y = 12
$$

### Step 3: Solve for $y$
Expand the equation:

$$
2y + 2 + 3y = 12
$$

Combine like terms:

$$
5y + 2 = 12
$$

Subtract 2 from both sides:

$$
5y = 10
$$

Divide by 5:

$$
y = 2
$$

### Step 4: Solve for $x$
Now substitute the value of $y$ back into Equation 3:

$$
x = 2 + 1
$$

$$
x = 3
$$

### Final Answer
The values that satisfy both equations are:

$$
x = 3, \quad y = 2
$$
